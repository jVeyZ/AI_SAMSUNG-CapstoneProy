Samsung Innovation Campus: Capstone Project Student’s Guide

1. Project Topic Guidelines

Core Requirements:

* Theme: Must be within the boundary of “Technology for Good”.
* Impact: Projects should address real-world problems and be of public interest and/or benefit.
* Demo: You are required to create an interactive demo of the AI component.

Discouraged Topics:

* Stock price forecasting, gambling/investment strategies, racing forecasts, etc.
* Projects where the primary value is entertainment.
* Projects where only a single individual or a specific company benefits.
* Projects that are purely theoretical or serve only to prove existing techniques.

Academic Approach:

* Go beyond the classroom: Do not limit your project to what was taught in class. You are highly encouraged to do your own background reading on the problem domain and advanced AI techniques.
* Be practical: Avoid reinventing the wheel. Find existing components and "glue" them together in an original, useful way. Don't make it too easy, but keep it realistic.

2. Team Formation

* Size: Each team must consist of exactly 3 members.
* Composition: Ideally, members should bring complementary skillsets and share a common interest.
* Method: Students are encouraged to associate freely. Instructors may intervene to help, merge, break up, or rearrange teams if necessary.

3. Project Schedule & Deadlines (2026)

* Plan Presentation: PowerPoint Presentation. Deadline: 13/07/2026
* First Prototype Presentation: PowerPoint Presentation. Deadline: 20/07/2026
* Final Report: Word Document. Soft deadline: 27/07/2026 | Hard deadline: 28/07/2026
* Final Presentation: PowerPoint Presentation. Deadline: 28/07/2026

4. Phase Details & Deliverables

Planning Phase:

* Action Plan: Describe what the team plans to achieve (goals, data collection methods, training techniques, visualization, etc.). Format: Word document (template available on Poliformat). Language of choice.
* Work Breakdown Structure (WBS): A highly granular breakdown of all tasks along with estimated time costs. This is crucial to ensure your team remains realistic and learns to estimate project timelines. Format: Excel sheet. Language of choice.
* Plan Presentation: Explain your action plan to the class. Format: 4 to 5 slides per team. Duration: 10 to 15 minute presentation + 5 minutes of Q&A. Language of choice.

Prototyping & Final Phase:

* First Prototype Presentation: Show your initial progress. Duration: 20-minute presentation + 10 minutes of Q&A. Language of choice.
* Final Report: Describe your core reasoning, development process, and results. These will be published in an official Samsung book. Format: Word document. Language to be determined.
* Final Presentation: Present your completed project. Samsung representatives will be present for reviews and awards. Duration: 20-minute presentation + 10 minutes of Q&A. Language of choice. Includes a group photo session, wrap-up ceremony, and awards.

5. Evaluation Criteria

The Capstone Project accounts for 60% of your total course grade.

* Idea (10%): Creativity / Impact. Instructors may also factor in student cross-evaluations.
* Application (25%): Maintenance / Utilization / Practicality.
* Result (25%): Efficiency / Performance / Maturity.
* Project Management (5%): Teamwork / Adaptability / Time Management.
* Presentation & Report (35%): Clarity / Effectiveness / Q&A.

6. Logistics & Classroom Advice

* Instructor Support: Instructors will be present in class and available for advice and high-level questions. However, they will not assist with direct coding or debugging.
* Attendance Policy: In-person classroom attendance is the default standard. Notify your teammates in advance if you cannot make it to class. If circumstances allow, class may end early—however, this does not mean there is no work to be done. Fully remote days are planned and will be communicated in advance if requested.
